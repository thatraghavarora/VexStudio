Game Overview

Genre: Horror Puzzle
Engine: Unreal Engine 5
Platform: PC (Windows)
Perspective: First Person
Status: Currently Under Development
Team Size: Solo Developer

Andhkaar (Hindi for "darkness") is a first-person horror puzzle game that combines intricate puzzle mechanics with a suspenseful horror atmosphere. Players must navigate dark, interconnected environments, solve environmental and inventory-based puzzles, and piece together a disturbing narrative — all while managing limited resources and avoiding lurking dangers.

Core Mechanics

Puzzle System

  • Environmental puzzles — manipulate objects, levers, and mechanisms in the world
  • Logic puzzles — pattern recognition, combination locks, and sequential challenges
  • Multi-step puzzles requiring items from different areas
  • Puzzle difficulty escalates as the player progresses deeper into the environment

Inventory System

  • Grid-based inventory with limited capacity — forces strategic item management
  • Item inspection — rotate and examine objects for hidden clues
  • Combine items to create new tools or keys
  • Key items vs. consumables — different storage priorities

Exploration & Horror

  • Non-linear exploration with gated progression through puzzle solving
  • Dynamic horror events — scripted and randomized scares
  • Limited light sources — flashlight with battery management
  • Environmental hazards that require observation and caution

Visual & Atmosphere Design

  • Emissive material effects for atmospheric, eerie glow on objects and surfaces
  • Heavy use of darkness and shadow — the player's flashlight is their primary sight tool
  • Volumetric fog and particle effects for environmental depth
  • Custom 3D assets and environment props modeled in Blender
  • Color palette: deep blacks, muted teals, and warm amber highlights for contrast
  • Post-processing: film grain, subtle chromatic aberration, and dynamic exposure

Level Design Philosophy

  • Interconnected rooms — shortcuts unlock as puzzles are solved
  • Environmental storytelling through visual details, notes, and object placement
  • Safe rooms for inventory management and narrative exposition
  • Vertical and horizontal exploration with hidden areas
  • Suspenseful pacing — quiet exploration punctuated by horror encounters

Technical Implementation

Unreal Engine 5 Blueprints Blender Inventory System Puzzle Logic Emissive Materials Interaction System Save/Load
  • Modular puzzle framework — puzzle components can be reused and reconfigured
  • Inventory system with UI built entirely in UMG (Unreal Motion Graphics)
  • Interactive object system using Blueprint interfaces
  • Save/Load system for persistent game state across sessions
  • Dynamic lighting with baked and real-time shadows for performance balance
